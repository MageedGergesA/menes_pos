# Wave 2 — "Will a chain trust it?" backlog

W1 made it legally sellable to one merchant. W2 makes it trustworthy to a
multi-unit operator: accountability, the money workflows chains expect, reach
(aggregators/reporting), and the offline engine. Ordered by value × tractability.

## 2A — Enterprise trust (builds directly on W1's cashier/audit)  ← START HERE
1. **Manager-approval workflow** for high-risk actions (void, refund, discount
   over a threshold, price override, no-sale). A cashier below the required role
   must get a supervisor/manager PIN; the approver is verified server-side and
   recorded in the audit trail. Primitive: `/w1/approve`.
2. **Swap the SUPERUSER `_env()` scaffold** in `sync.py`/`w1.py` for the bridge's
   real `_api_env()` (a POS user) — closes the review's HIGH item.
3. **Unreconciled-reversals queue** — surface `payment.reversal` /
   `flagged_manual` events on the manager dashboard so a stranded card charge
   can't be silently forgotten (closes the CRITICAL-#1 residual).

## 2B — Core money workflows
4. **Split bill** — by seat and by item, each split payable independently.
5. **Exchange flow** — even-exchange + price-difference; today only refund exists.
6. **Refund reason codes** — structured reasons on every refund (feeds reporting).
7. **One-tap cash** — remove the 2nd tender tap for exact cash (Fitts/Hick).

## 2C — Reach & integrations
8. **Aggregator order injection** — Talabat / Jahez / HungerStation orders land as
   POS orders + fire to the kitchen (huge share of MENA F&B volume).
9. **Reporting exports** — CSV/PDF, scheduled email, and an accounting/GL bridge
   (finance teams reject dashboard-only).
10. **Hardware layer** — ESC/POS printer, cash drawer, scanner, KDS printer
    fallback (needs physical devices to certify).

## 2D — Offline engine
11. **Sync apply-path** — implement the push apply (upsert by uuid) + the
    commutative inventory/loyalty delta-merge from `docs/SYNC.md` (the transport
    + idempotency layer already exists).

## Definition of "W2 done" (recommendation)
2A + 2B + reporting (9). Aggregators, hardware, and the offline engine are each
their own project; sequence them by the specific merchant/chain you're closing.
